Harira 

Ingredients
  • 200 g of chickpeas
  • 100 g lentils
  • 900 g of meat (beef or lamb)
  • 800 g tomato
  • 50 g of tomato paste
  • 300 g finely chopped celery
  • 200 g chopped fresh cilantro
  • 200 g chopped fresh parsley
  • 400 g onion
  • 1 g of saffron
  • 2 g of turmeric
  • 2 g of pepper
  • 20 g of coarse salt
  • 1 cube of lamb or beef broth
  • 7 cl olive oil
  • 4 liters of water
  • 25 g of vermicelli
  • 50 g of cornstarch
  • 5 g rancid butter (traditional smen) optional
method
  • The day before, soak the chickpeas and lentils separately in cold water
  • Remove the skin from the chickpeas and drain the lentils. Reserve.
  • Cut the meat into small dice (1x1cm). Lump, seed and dice the tomatoes as well. Reserve.
  • Peel the celery stalks, cut them lengthwise. Then mince them to a thickness of about 3mm.
  • In a pot, heat a drizzle of olive oil and brown the pieces of meat. Add the onions, celery and chopped herbs. Saut√© for 2 to 3 minutes. Stir in the tomato, tomato paste, saffron, pepper, turmeric, chickpeas, lentils and meat broth. Stir.
  • Wet, salt and bring to a boil. Cover and cook over low heat for 1 hour. Check the cooking.
  • Mix the cornstarch in a bowl of water. Add to the soup while stirring.
  • Pour in the vermicelli and cook for another 3 minutes without covering. Adjust the seasoning and the binding.
  • Serve hot, sprinkle with chopped fresh herbs.
